# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Movie watchers who want a fast personal place to decide what to watch next and keep a small, meaningful record of what they have seen. They are likely using the app between recommendations, while browsing titles, or after finishing a movie.

## Product Purpose

Framekeep helps a user search OMDB, save movies to a watchlist, move watched titles into a rated log, and keep that state locally across refreshes. Success means the app feels immediate, trustworthy, and polished while keeping the core workflow obvious on mobile and desktop.

## Brand Personality

Considered, cinematic, precise. The interface should feel high-end without looking theatrical or over-designed.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI-dashboard styling, empty hero copy, over-rounded glass cards, purple gradient-heavy layouts, stock-looking decoration, cluttered movie database pages, and interfaces that hide the actual task behind marketing content.

## Design Principles

- Make the movies the visual lead through posters, compact metadata, and direct actions.
- Keep the primary loop visible: search, queue, log, rate, and edit.
- Use density with breathing room so the app feels useful rather than decorative.
- Favor tactile, predictable controls over novelty.
- Preserve confidence through clear loading, duplicate, empty, and error states.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-visible focus, semantic headings for route announcements, touch targets of at least 40px where practical, readable mobile layouts, and reduced-motion-safe interaction polish.
